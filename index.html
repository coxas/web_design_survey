Welcome to the Web Design Survey Questionnaire.

Thank you for taking the time to complete this survey. We expect it will take around 10 minutes to complete. If you have questions about this survey, please contact alyssacox@uchicago.edu.

Data Disclosure Statement

This survey is being administered as part of a study on web design preferences. We are researching the correlation between certain demographic characteristics and preferences regarding several fundamental concepts in web design.

"Web Design" is a term that encompasses many aspects of visual design. In this study, we are using the phrase to refer to discrete aspects of a webpage, including visual, audio, and interacive components. Each question in the web design portion of the survey asks about one specific component.

For this survey we would like you to think about simple web sites, not web services or web applications. Web services and applications usually provide some service to the user and require a high level of user participation or interaction. Examples of web services and applications include Facebook, GMail, Amazon, or Google. We do not want you to think about these types of web services when answering the questions about design preferences in our survey (questions 13 - 22).

In contrast, simple web sites are mostly for users to view and read. Examples might include someone's personal webpage with information about themselves, a political campaign website, a business website, or a blog.

Think about some simple web sites that you have visited lately. What did they look like? Can you visualize them in your head? Do you remember what you liked or disliked about the site? These types of observations will be helpful when answering our survey questions.

Survey Instructions

This survey consists of two parts.

Part I asks about demographic characteristics of the respondent. All of the questions in Part I include the option "Prefer not to respond." If you are uncomfortable answering the question, you may choose this response.

Part II asks about the respondent's opinions on a number of core elements of web design. Definitions and visual examples are included when relevant.

The questions for Part I and Part II are shown on different pages. Use the "next" and "back" buttons to navigate through the questionnaire. At the end of the questionnaire, you be see a "Submit" button. Clicking this button will submit your data and the survey will be completed.

Note: None of the questions in this survey are required.
